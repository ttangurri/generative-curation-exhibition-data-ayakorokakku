# generative-curation-exhibition-data-ayakorokakku

### A Generative Curation Project Based on Exhibition Data

## 1. Project Overview

This project is a generative curation experiment based on the exhibition data of  
Ayako Rokkaku’s solo exhibition *Breathing with the Chaos*, held at the Total Museum of Contemporary Art.

Rather than treating the exhibition as a closed and finalized outcome,  
the project approaches it as a dataset that can be re-interpreted and re-structured.  
Its goal is not to use exhibition data merely as input for generative models,  
but to translate images, texts, spatial configurations, and sensory impressions  
into **rules** and **structures**, through which a **secondary exhibition** can be generated.

The project explores the possibility of **generative curation**,  
where curatorial logic itself becomes a generative framework.

---

## 2. Problem Statement


- the curatorial logic of the exhibition,
- the relationships between artworks,
- and the rhythm and atmosphere produced by space, movement, and sensory experience.

Under the guiding premise  
**“The exhibition has ended, but the data remains alive,”**  
this project shifts attention to the pre-generative stage and asks:

- What should be considered data?
- What elements should remain transformable?
- What must be preserved throughout the process?

These questions are addressed through structural and rule-based design.

---

## 3. Methodology: Designing Structures Before Generation

The core methodology includes:
- Decomposing exhibition data into sensory components  
  (rhythm, gesture, color, density, affect, etc.)
- Translating sensory elements into **rules** and **constraints**
- Designing **meta-prompts** rather than single prompts
- Recording the conditions and logic of generation rather than focusing on outputs

Through this approach, the exhibition is understood not as a single answer,  
but as a structure capable of “breathing” in multiple configurations.

---

## 4. Exhibition Context and Authorization

This project is not a speculative exercise alone.  
It is developed in parallel with a planned physical exhibition in Germany,  
based on formal authorization for secondary creation from the artist  
Ayako Rokkaku and institutional collaboration with the Total Museum of Contemporary Art.

The dataset used in this project includes:
- Exhibition images and curatorial texts provided by the museum
- Administrative records related to exhibition planning and operation
- Environmental records such as weather data during the exhibition period
- Visitor-related records collected in anonymized and aggregated form

These materials are treated not as static references,  
but as active components that inform the generative curatorial structure  
of the secondary exhibition.

## 6. On the Generated Outputs

The visual outputs generated in this project are not treated as autonomous artworks.  
They function as evidence that a given structure or rule set is operating.

Accordingly, the repository prioritizes:
- how specific rules produce certain variations,
- how those rules embody curatorial logic,
- and how structural decisions shape exhibition possibilities.

Aesthetic completion is secondary to structural clarity.

---

## 7. Limitations and Future Directions

As a project developed within the constraints of a short-term hackathon,  
this work has several limitations:
- the subjective nature of sensory interpretation,
- the experimental character of rule design,
- and the gap between generated structures and lived exhibition experience.

Future extensions may include:
- ontological modeling of exhibition data,
- connections to archival description standards such as RiC(Records in Contexts_Archival Scinece fields),
- and structures that allow active participation by visitors.

---

## 8. Project Statement

This project begins as a research-driven experiment,  
but is developed with the explicit intention of being realized as a physical exhibition.

Rather than focusing on prompt engineering,  
it emphasizes the structuring of curatorial thinking.

> Structure over results,  
> rules over generation,  
> exhibition conditions over individual artworks.
